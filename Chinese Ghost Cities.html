Index

David was sitting on the cold unclean floor smoking pot. No idea what time it is, heavy curtains protecting him from the consideration of when he slept last and how long for. All the days are together, one unbroken stretch of time free from the rising and setting of the sun, an eternity, or a timeless present.

He thought about Carl, the bastard, the way he’d come in friendly and once he’s left you notice things in your house gone. Or always asking for a loan. Something part of you ripped out, a hole in the home. Ava was like this too even though she wouldn’t rob you. She’d cajole and convince, flatter and then you’d find out what she’s said when you were gone. What she’d done with the love extended. Ava and Carl, really, the same person. A flash of an image, bringing the faces to memory. He pulls up his phone and scrolls through his photos, comparing their faces. Look at how similar they look. Pulls up messages and scrolls. He decides they sound the same. But he’d seen them in the same room together, so how does that work? More a puzzle than a contradiction requiring rejection of his theory, he’d seized on this idea and extrapolates. He wonders if there aren’t more than 1000 real people in the world. The thought of this unpopulated world makes him feel simultaneous awe and deep aloneness, but it’s beautiful. Laying down, he remembers the radio playing. Feeling hollow and full of the radio emissions it’s the only thing inside of him. Becoming the music it orders his perception, the rhythm is thought and feeling according to it, all sensation. Sad song so he is this particular sorrow as it is drawn from beat to beat, identity expressed experienced as the shifting of melody. The song carries him along in the unpopulated world, he sees quickly succeeding images of empty cities, expansive plains, vast dry deserts sparsely inhabited fused to his idea, looking around the room where he’s alone affirming the reality of his aloneness, affirming the story the music tells, that there are less than 1000 people. He’s seen the stories of unpopulated Chinese ghost cities on Twitter, built to attract without a preexisting population and decides this too must be the truth of the greater world but they’re only telling us so much.

A knock on the door and it’s Ava when he goes to check. Alarmed and cautious, revealing nothing of his earlier realizations because something is holding him back, he can’t quite say what, but he knows he’s not supposed to share things like this. A violation of a felt, intuited rule. He lets her in. She’s wearing a bright red sweatshirt, commanded total attention so fully he felt and became red. The woven texture wobbles in place, visceral. She’s rendered in high fidelity, her sleek black hair just as consuming and fused with the assaulting red fused with the eyes and fused with feeling, possessed of an aura seizing his focus totally. Feeling in the eyes, a situation that he would be at pains to describe if asked, and something necessarily private about it. When she turns around to descend the stairs the bright and ominous red-black sleek-soft and assaultive and, too, portending something very secret, a promise of revelation that is deep in extension and total blackness. The shining gleam of the dim light in the staircase red-black every microscopic fiber and hair-strand stands out individual. Timid light with bold darkness giving spirit and feeling to the light. He follows her feels like he’s descending a tunnel into the pits of the earth. The light-dark hits him with blaring intensity through shared illumination and consuming shadow. Feels like he could watch forever.

Inside, Ava takes a seat on the couch. She’s waving and the red-black texture taking on kaleidoscopic tones now, defined at the level of the fabric of the shirt, appears she’s approaching while never getting closer. Quiet and shifts her head the straight arrow gaze piercing David deep inside and he can feel. David, captive to, wants to find the truth of it. Thinks he can keep looking forever as it keeps showing its endless self deeper and more revealing. She asks for a hit softly and breaking the gaze and David dutifully obliges. Breathing in deep and on exhale, cascading smoke swirling like tidepools in the ocean. Who knows how deep the water is but his gaze is firmly fixed on the ceiling and entering inside of it also again felt inside. Ava asks, are you alright, you seem like you’re thinking about something. Yeah I’m fine, he says he knows he’s lying distantly, captivated by the senses. Ava is shifting, her form is unclear and it contains so much now, shadows don’t definitively determine but instead infinitely suggest change the depth of a trench, if you tried to pin it down to something concrete you’d come up with nothing but a swallowing vacuum.

Remembering Carl, and suddenly overtaken by an intimation that Ava is a devil-figure. The qualities of the perception become defining, mental, but on the cusp of physical realization. The time Ava asked for a hundred dollars and then he’d heard what she’d said about him not a week later, Ava and Carl laughing together, Carl coming over and fifty dollars gone missing. Ava now looking thin, shadowy ghostlike, stretching to the ceiling disappearing into ether. Unable to decide where she stops and ends the sense again shows David Carl and Ava who cannot tell you what they are. Nearly seeing, the sense on the fringe of perception, Ava stands, outline simmering and unclear can not tell the thin fringe from the stuff it bounds. Where does she start and end, yet this in front of his face, confouding by being more real than anything prior. Appearing as one thing and another but being something else, the shifting lack of definiteness the truth of the world, uncertain, unable to comprehend and ultimately it’s a whole lot of nothing. She smokes and dissolves but he’s resolving her into successions of comprehensions, the red-black and the devilishness of Carl and the dim shadow-light at once but, apprehension shifting to apprehension, a story telling itself, but still constant, through successions, unreality suggesting reality inseparably in turn all at once. Maybe there are fewer than 1000 people, maybe just one, or two.

A couple hours in near silence, David consumed privately, Ava decides to leave. Again alone but knows that with Ava he was still alone. Wonders about the truth of his body, hollow, nothing, in extension with Ava. The music is gone and the wind blows through him empty as he is. He feels his nonexistence deep inside, dead if he was ever alive, inhabiting wandering the uninhabited world. He sees what he knows.
