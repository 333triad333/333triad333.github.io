Just sitting around watching TV with her could be unbearable. I'd feel the desire to escape in each passing second and it would sink into my skin. Hours of bare enduring the restlessness, anxiety without making a show of it. Not just not showing it but actively pretending otherwise. The dishonesty and attendant guilt, inseparable from the primary desire to flee, mixed, just one awful heavy hell altogether. She'd put her arm around me. I'd tense and suppress it so she couldn't feel. The anger concealed the stress and I'd smother this too, pack it all down.

For too long I was too much of a coward to end it and it didn't end because I found the courage. The end came only because of a final failure of self-control on top of the cowardice; one night I lost it, not even over anything particularly significant, it was an ordinary demand, but they all were, I guess they piled on top of each other until the whole thing blew.

It was bad from the beginning, this girl showed interest and I went along with it out of curiosity, but I never developed the feelings I should have for her, I limped along through the thing, adapting to the shape of her personage, she'd grow into my absence and I’d get smaller, the whole artifice holding me tighter and smaller and more rigid. She'd pour affection on me and all I felt was sadness, smallness, the guilt, the obligation to keep hiding. I had made myself a captive and she was still innocent. She was basically a good person and I wasn't.

The innocence and idealism she showed, her unselfconscious giving in love, sharpened the contours of me and her, that was something I could never do. It was beautiful and I admired it but I knew I could never meet it, not with her, maybe not with anyone, I don't know what it means to be in love. I admired the genuineness, the unguardedness, that someone could be like that. I loved the tenderness but this isn't the same as loving her, I know that.

I had grown resentful of keeping to myself. It happened over the phone. I don't even remember what it was about, but it was political. I contradicted what she said because I didn't agree. At this point she was used to my docility and what I said didn't get through because she was used to not hearing me. It escalated, I got visibly angry and hung up on her. When she called back she said, I think we should break up. I know she wanted resistance, for me to show her that I needed her or wanted her, to push back. I didn't; I just agreed. She came over to drop off one of my books a few days later and I still remember her crying in my bed and I just watched her.

I don't know if candidness is always a virtue but I can't help myself, I want to tell the truth. I find absolution in it, but maybe this is, and especially in this case, a further cruelty. After we broke up I told her how I felt and I know it was shocking and painful. I felt right confessing and I didn't expect the pain and shock. I told her I didn’t think she really knew who I was and that my late attempts at exposure were rebuffed. That I didn't feel seen or valued. She said, so the whole thing was fake, oh my God. Her reaction frightened me and I know that was her fear I was feeling. I saw myself now not as telling the truth but as someone who could cause terror in another person.
